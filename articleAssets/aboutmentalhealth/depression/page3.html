Depression can result from various factors such as:

  • environmental and situational factors related to relationship difficulties, financial problems, or work stressors
  • neurochemical changes in the brain from injury or genetic vulnerability
  • psychological factors in the area of negative thinking patterns and poor emotional regulation

While it is normal to feel sad when we face unpleasant or adverse events, people who are clinically depressed experiences more lasting sadness even after the negative event has passed for a long time.
