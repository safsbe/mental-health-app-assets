TREATMENT

Fortunately, anxiety disorders are treatable. With early diagnosis and proper treatment, the brain can adapt, and many patients recover.

Around 60 to 70 percent of people improve and can lead normal lives. Treatment aims to reduce and eventually eliminate the symptoms of anxiety disorders while improving quality of life.

By medications: Antidepressants can help control anxiety with minimal side effects, even with long-term use. Benzodiazepines may be used for short periods.

By non-medication treatments: Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is one of the most effective treatments for anxiety. This includes exposure therapy, where people face their fears gradually.

Other ways to help manage anxiety include learning stress management techniques like relaxation and mindfulness.
