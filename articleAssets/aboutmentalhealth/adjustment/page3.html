The primary cause of an adjustment disorder is a recent stressful life event, though individuals respond to stress in different ways. Factors like temperament, coping skills, past experiences, developmental stage, and social support systems play a role in how we manage stress. Stress can vary in duration, intensity, and impact.

COMMON SOURCES OF STRESS

  • The end of a friendship or romantic relationship
  • Early onset of puberty
  • Experiencing an accident
  • Receiving a medical diagnosis
  • Parental separation or divorce, loss of a family member, or family conflict
  • Moving to a new home, community, or country
  • Stressful school experiences, such as bullying, major exams, or changing schools
