SYMPTOMS

The main symptoms of an adjustment disorder in children and youths can be emotional, behavioral, or both. These may include:

  • Feelings of sadness, hopelessness, worry, fear, irritability, or anger
  • Unexplained body aches, poor sleep, or loss of appetite
  • Clinginess to key figures (fear of separation)
  • Withdrawal from family and friends
  • Problems with school or schoolwork
  • Truancy, fighting, or acting out behaviors
  • Self-harming behaviors
  • Thoughts of or attempts at suicide

While these symptoms can be as distressing as those of depression, anxiety, or behavioral issues, they are usually less severe.
