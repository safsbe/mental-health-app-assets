FACTORS THAT CAUSES IT

OCD can be influenced by several factors:

  • Genetics: OCD can sometimes run in families, meaning it can be inherited from a parent.

  • Biological factors: Research suggests that OCD may be linked to an imbalance of serotonin, a chemical in the brain.

  • Life changes: Major life events, like starting a new job or having a child, can increase stress and responsibilities, which might trigger OCD.

  • Behavioral factors: People who are very organized, neat, or like to be in control from a young age may be more likely to develop OCD.

  • Personal experiences: Experiencing a traumatic event, like getting a severe rash from touching harmful substances, could lead to compulsions, such as excessive hand washing.
