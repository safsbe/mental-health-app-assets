SYMPTOMS

Burnout is marked by a combination of key symptoms that affect mental, emotional, and physical well-being.

Exhaustion: At the core of burnout is extreme fatigueâ€”physical, mental, and emotional. It can stem from long hours, overwhelming expectations, and a lack of autonomy or support. Even simple tasks feel draining and causes our focus and motivation to plummet.

Cynicism (Depersonalization): A sense of detachment from work, colleagues, and responsibilities. People may feel indifferent, negative, or emotionally distant due to conflicts, unfair treatment, or work overload.

Inefficacy: Burnout can lead to feelings of incompetence, lack of achievement, and reduced productivity. With limited resources, unclear expectations, or insufficient recognition, even seasoned individuals may doubt their skills.

Recognizing these signs early is vital for preventing burnout and regaining a healthy balance in work and life.
