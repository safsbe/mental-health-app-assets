WHAT IS IT

Bipolar disorder is characterised by extreme mood swings from being very happy and energetic (maniac episode) to very sad and demotivated (depressive episode).

In 2019, approximately 1 in 150 adults (40 million people, or 0.53% of the global population) were living with bipolar disorder. While the prevalence of bipolar disorder among men and women is approximately equal, available data indicate that women are more often diagnosed.

The exact cause of bipolar is yet known, but can be triggered by extreme stress, genetic or chemical influence or life-changing events. It can occur to anyone at any point in time.
