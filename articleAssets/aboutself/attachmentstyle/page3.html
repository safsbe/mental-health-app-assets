At that time, many psychoanalytic theorists believed these behaviors were immature defense mechanisms meant to suppress emotional pain. However, Bowlby believed that building a strong bond with specific person is an essential part of survival.

With the person we are attached to, we desire to be near them (proximity maintenance) and be able to return to them for comfort and safety when faced with challenge or threat (safe haven). The safe space provided by the attachment figure allows us to explore our environment and uncertainties with more confidence (secure base) but can cause us anxiety if the attachment figure is absent (separation distress).
